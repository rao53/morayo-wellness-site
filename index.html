    About Morayo Wellness Initiative

    Our Story

    Morayo Wellness Initiative is a nonprofit founded in 2025, born out of a personal mission to ensure every woman and girl in Nigeria has access to high-quality, trauma-informed mental health care. We believe healing is a right, not a luxury. Our name, Morayo, means "I see joy again," reflecting our commitment to help survivors of abuse, early marriage, and pregnancy trauma rediscover strength and hope. We are dedicated to making trauma-informed mental health care accessible to every woman and girl—whether in person at trusted local clinics or online via secure video sessions—and integrating therapy seamlessly into daily life, not just in crisis moments.

    Who Can Apply for Therapy Funding?

    We support girls and women in Nigeria (starting in Lagos) who have experienced or are currently experiencing:

    Sexual abuse or violence
    Early or forced marriage
    Unwanted or early pregnancy
    Postpartum depression
    Emotional trauma or grief
    Mental health crisis (anxiety, depression, PTSD)
    Abandonment, isolation, or untreated depression
    Domestic or intimate partner violence

    We prioritize individuals with no insurance, support, or financial means to access care.

    Meet Our Founder

    Mariam Motunrayo Sulaimon

    She has extensive experience working in mental health research and digital mental health innovation. Her work has focused on developing interventions that improve access to culturally responsive care and address the structural inequities and social determinants of health that prevent people especially women and girls from getting the support they deserve.

    She believes mental health is a right, not a privilege, and is passionate about building community-centered solutions that restore hope and dignity.

    Mariam founded Morayo Wellness Initiative to help every woman and girl reclaim her joy and well-being, free from stigma or financial barriers. Her vision is to expand Morayo's reach over time supporting and funding therapy for 30–50 women and girls in Nigeria per year after the pilot phase, and scaling services as community needs grow.

    The Challenge

    Access Gap

    Fewer than 10% of Nigerians in need receive any mental health support. This gap is even wider for women facing abuse or trauma.

    Cultural Stigma

    Mental health is still a taboo topic in many Nigerian communities. Shame, myths, and misinformation discourage help-seeking.

    Practical Barriers

    Traffic congestion, unsafe commutes, and transport costs can make in-person therapy out of reach.

    Digital Divide

    Around 45% of Nigerian women have internet access—higher in Lagos (66%)—but many still rely on basic phones, limiting digital reach.

    Our Approach

    We meet women where they are—physically and emotionally:

    Two Ways to Connect

    Clients choose between private in-person sessions at trusted clinics or teletherapy (video, phone, or SMS) from home.

    Flexible Scheduling

    Evening and weekend slots available, with 60–90 minute sessions.

    Community Partnerships

    We work with schools, faith centers, NGOs, and community leaders to build trust and find those most in need.

    Survivor-Centered Design

    Women with lived experience co-create programs, materials, and decisions to ensure services meet real needs.

    Our Vision

    What We Offer

    At Morayo Wellness Initiative, our network of Nigerian therapists provides professional counseling to women and girls for free or at low cost.

    In-Person Therapy

    Safe, confidential sessions at vetted local clinics.

    Teletherapy

    Secure video sessions from the comfort of your home.

    Flexible Scheduling

    Evening/weekend appointments; 60 minutes per session.

    Holistic Support

    Digital wellness tools, downloadable self-help guides, and transport stipends if needed.

    Pilot & Testing

    In 2025, we launched our pilot in two Lagos LGAs:

    Agege

    Densely populated with high GBV and teen pregnancy rates.

    Ikorodu

    A mixed urban/rural area with limited trauma care access.

    Pilot Details

    Who

    10–15 women/girls per LGA, each receiving 4–8 sessions.

    How

    Clients chose online or in-person based on safety and comfort.

    Partners

    2–3 licensed trauma-informed therapists per LGA.

    When

    July–November 2025.

    We collect feedback from clients and therapists at every stage to refine our model. Our evidence-driven approach ensures Morayo evolves with real community needs.

    Why This Matters

    Huge Treatment Gap

    WHO data shows fewer than 10% of Nigerians with mental health needs get care. Most women affected by trauma go unsupported.

    Digital Boost

    Nigerian studies show reminders and digital platforms improve therapy attendance and reduce missed visits.

    Women's Connectivity

    66% of Lagos women use the internet, far above national averages.

    Inclusivity

    Offering phone and SMS counseling expands access to women with basic phones or safety concerns.

    We combine technology with community support to make therapy truly accessible.

    Evidence & Impact

    Our work is rooted in research, data, and real community feedback:

    Access Gap

    Only 10% of Nigerians needing mental health care receive it (WHO/NHAct).

    Retention

    SMS/phone reminders boost attendance from 68% to 90%.

    Digital Readiness

    66% of Lagos women use the internet, enabling teletherapy access.

    Inclusivity

    Phone/SMS options reach those without smartphones; satisfaction remains high.

    Outcomes

    Studies show teletherapy reduces depression and improves life quality.

    Trust Channels

    Female health workers increase uptake by up to 40% when integrated in referral/outreach.

    We track attendance, outcomes, and satisfaction to improve our services continuously.

    FAQ

    Who can access services?

    Any woman or girl in Nigeria affected by trauma. No income, insurance, or paperwork barrier.

    How do I choose between online and in-person?

    Choose what's safest and most convenient. Both offer quality, confidential care.

    Can I get help without a smartphone?

    Yes. Phone or in-person sessions are always available.

    What if I can't afford transportation?

    We offer transport stipends—just mention this in your intake form.

    Are sessions confidential?

    Absolutely. All therapists follow professional ethical codes.

    How long are sessions?

    Standard sessions are 60 minutes, with 90 minutes available if needed.

    How do we know it works?

    Research shows reminders and digital tools improve therapy access and outcomes. We collect session data and feedback to refine our services in real time.
