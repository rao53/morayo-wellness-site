    About Morayo Wellness Initiative

    Morayo Wellness Initiative is a nonprofit founded in 2025, born out of a personal mission to ensure every woman and girl in Nigeria has access to high-quality, trauma-informed mental health care. Our name, Morayo, means "I see joy again," reflecting our commitment to help survivors rediscover strength and hope.

    Our Mission

    We believe healing is a right, not a luxury. We subsidize therapy, expand reach through online and clinic-based counseling, empower survivor voices, and work to normalize mental health care.

    Who We Serve

    We support girls and women in Nigeria who have experienced sexual abuse, early marriage, pregnancy trauma, postpartum depression, domestic violence, or other mental health challenges.

    Our Approach

    We meet women where they are—physically and emotionally—offering both in-person and teletherapy options with flexible scheduling and survivor-centered design.

    The Challenge We're Addressing

    Access Gap

    Fewer than 10% of Nigerians in need receive any mental health support. This gap is even wider for women facing abuse or trauma.

    Cultural Stigma

    Mental health is still a taboo topic in many Nigerian communities. Shame, myths, and misinformation discourage help-seeking.

    Practical Barriers

    Traffic congestion, unsafe commutes, and transport costs can make in-person therapy out of reach.

    Digital Divide

    Around 45% of Nigerian women have internet access—higher in Lagos (66%)—but many still rely on basic phones, limiting digital reach.

    Evidence & Impact

    Our work is rooted in research, data, and real community feedback:

    Access Gap

    Only 10% of Nigerians needing mental health care receive it (WHO/NHAct).

    Retention

    SMS/phone reminders boost attendance from 68% to 90%.

    Digital Readiness

    66% of Lagos women use the internet, enabling teletherapy access.

    Inclusivity

    Phone/SMS options reach those without smartphones; satisfaction remains high.

    Outcomes

    Studies show teletherapy reduces depression and improves life quality.

    Trust Channels

    Female health workers increase uptake by up to 40% when integrated in referral/outreach.

    We track attendance, outcomes, and satisfaction to improve our services continuously.
