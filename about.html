    About Us

    Our journey to make mental health care accessible for every woman and girl in Nigeria

    Our Story

    Morayo Wellness Initiative is a nonprofit founded in 2025, born out of a personal mission to ensure every woman and girl in Nigeria has access to high-quality, trauma-informed mental health care. We believe healing is a right, not a luxury.

    Our name, Morayo, means "I see joy again," reflecting our commitment to help survivors of abuse, early marriage, and pregnancy trauma rediscover strength and hope. We are dedicated to making trauma-informed mental health care accessible to every woman and girl—whether in person at trusted local clinics or online via secure video sessions—and integrating therapy seamlessly into daily life, not just in crisis moments.

    Who Can Apply for Therapy Funding?

    We support girls and women in Nigeria (starting in Lagos) who have experienced or are currently experiencing:

    Sexual abuse or violence
    Early or forced marriage
    Unwanted or early pregnancy
    Postpartum depression
    Emotional trauma or grief
    Mental health crisis (e.g., anxiety, depression, PTSD)
    Abandonment, isolation, or untreated depression
    Domestic or intimate partner violence

    We prioritize individuals with no insurance, support, or financial means to access care.

    The Challenge We're Addressing

    Access Gap

    Fewer than 10% of Nigerians in need receive any mental health support. This gap is even wider for women facing abuse or trauma.

    Cultural Stigma

    Mental health is still a taboo topic in many Nigerian communities. Shame, myths, and misinformation discourage help-seeking.

    Practical Barriers

    Traffic congestion, unsafe commutes, and transport costs can make in-person therapy out of reach.

    Digital Divide

    Around 45% of Nigerian women have internet access—higher in Lagos (66%)—but many still rely on basic phones, limiting digital reach.

    Our Approach

    We meet women where they are—physically and emotionally:

    Two Ways to Connect

    Clients choose between private in-person sessions at trusted clinics or secure video teletherapy from home.

    In-Person Sessions Video Therapy

    Flexible Scheduling

    Evening and weekend slots available, with 60–90 minute sessions.

    Community Partnerships

    We work with schools, faith centers, NGOs, and community leaders to build trust and find those most in need.

    Survivor-Centered Design

    Women with lived experience co-create programs, materials, and decisions to ensure services meet real needs.
